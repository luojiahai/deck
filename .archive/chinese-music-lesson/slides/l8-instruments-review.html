Instrument 1

古筝

gǔzhēng
古筝 image

古筝 gǔzhēng

Chinese zither

•

21 (or more) strings

•

Plucked with picks on fingers

•

Over 2500 years of history

•

古 = ancient, 筝 = zither

Listen to the sound — what Western instrument does it remind you of? (Harp)

4 / 14
Instrument 2

二胡

èrhú
二胡 image

二胡 èrhú

Chinese two-stringed fiddle

•

2 strings (二 = two)

•

Played with a bow

•

Very expressive, vocal-like sound

Compare with violin — similar but different! (Strings + bow, but held differently)

5 / 14
Instrument 3

琵琶

pípá
琵琶 image

琵琶 pípá

Chinese lute

•

Pear-shaped body, 4 strings

•

Played vertically

•

Name from technique: 琵 (forward pluck) + 琶 (backward pluck)

•

Onomatopoeic name!

6 / 14
Instrument 4

笛子

dízi
笛子 image

笛子 dízi

Chinese bamboo flute

•

Made of bamboo

•

Has a special vibrating membrane

•

Gives a buzzy, bright, unique sound

The vibrating membrane (笛膜 dí mó) is what makes the dízi sound so distinctive!

Grammar Review

3种语法复习

sān zhǒng yǔfǎ fùxí
正在

她正在弹古筝。

She is playing guzheng right now.

Action in progress

都

二胡、琵琶，他都会。

He can play both erhu and pipa.

"All / both"

一边

他一边弹琵琶一边唱歌。

He plays pipa while singing.

Simultaneous actions

Can you make a NEW sentence for each pattern using the instruments we just learned?

用你学过的乐器造三个新句子!
