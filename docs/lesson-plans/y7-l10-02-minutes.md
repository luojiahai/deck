## Lesson 2: Minutes (分) — X点Y分

**Option: C — Quick pace, 4 skill sprints**

### Learning Intention
We are learning to tell time with hours AND minutes in Chinese.

### Success Criteria
- I can say minutes after the hour using 分 (用分说几点几分)
- I can read a clock and say the time in Chinese (看钟表说中文时间)
- I can use 零 correctly for times like 3:05 (正确使用零)

### Materials Needed
- Projector/TV
- A4 paper for written work
- Pre-printed "Find Your Partner" cards (half clock faces, half Chinese text)
- Timer visible on projector for sprints

---

### Lesson Sequence

#### Review (0–8 min)
**What:** O'clock times + 两点 rule from Lesson 1.

**How:** Finger voting rapid-fire. Teacher projects clock faces. Students hold up fingers to show the hour: clock at 3:00 → 3 fingers, clock at 2:00 → 2 fingers. Then choral response: teacher points to clock, class says the time. Include 2:00 twice. Cold call one student: "Why is this 两点 and not 二点?"

**Connection:** "Last lesson: o'clock. Today: minutes. By the end of this lesson you'll be able to tell almost any time someone asks."

#### Learning Intention & Success Criteria (8–10 min)
Display. Read aloud. "Today is fast-paced — 5 quick sprints. Stay focused, move fast."

#### I Do (10–18 min) — Compact, gets straight to it

##### New Vocabulary
| 汉字 | 拼音 | English |
|---|---|---|
| 分 | fēn | minute(s) |
| 零 | líng | zero (for single-digit minutes) |

##### Radical Spotlight (Textbook p85): 土 and 寸

The textbook teaches 6 radicals in Lesson 10. Today we introduce the first 2. Show on projector:

| 部首 | Name | Meaning | Related character |
|---|---|---|---|
| 土 | tǔ | soil; earth | 地 (dì — ground) → 地铁 (subway, coming in Lesson 12!) |
| 寸 | cùn | unit; inch | 时 (time, seen in traditional 時間) |

Students copy each radical into their notebook. "These radicals are building blocks — recognising them helps you break down new characters." 30 seconds each.

##### Pattern: X点Y分
Formula on projector: **X + 点 + Y + 分**

Model with clock images:
- 三点十分 (3:10) — straightforward
- 五点零五分 (5:05) — **零 is required here!**
- 七点二十分 (7:20)
- 十二点零一分 (12:01) — yes, 零一分

**对比 (contrast) on projector:**

| Without 零 | With 零 |
|---|---|
| 三点五分 ❌ — sounds like 3:50? | 三点零五分 ✅ |
| 八点三分 ❌ | 八点零三分 ✅ |
| 六点十分 ✅ — no 零 needed (10 is two digits) | |

Rule: **If minutes are 1–9, insert 零 after 点.** If minutes are 10+, no 零.

##### CFU Checkpoints
- "4:07 — do we need 零?" Thumbs up = yes, thumbs down = no. (Yes: 四点零七分)
- "8:15 — do we need 零?" (No: 八点十五分 — 15 is two digits)
- "Write 3:05 on your paper. Hold it up." (三点零五分 — scan for 三分 error)

#### Flexible Practice (18–43 min) — 5 Sprints

##### Sprint 1: Lightning Listen (5 min)
Teacher says 8 times in Chinese, one every 20 seconds. Students write the DIGITS on paper. After each one: "Hold it up!" Quick scan. Times: 3:10, 5:05, 12:20, 7:03, 11:15, 2:07 (两! not 二), 6:30, 9:01.

- **Scaffold:** First 3, formula visible. Then hide it.
- **Extension:** Fluent students write both digits AND Chinese for each.
- **OTR:** Paper hold-up — every student responds every time.

##### Sprint 2: Find Your Partner (6 min)
Each student gets a card — half have clock faces, half have Chinese time expressions. Students walk around saying their time in Chinese. Goal: find the person with the matching card. When found, both sit together. First 5 pairs win. After all paired: each pair stands and says their time together (choral).

- **Setup needed:** Pre-printed cards — prepare before class.

##### Sprint 3: Error Hunt (5 min)
Projector shows 6 Chinese time expressions. Students write "✓" or "✗" on paper for each. If ✗, write the correction.

1. 两点零五分 ✓ (correct)
2. 二点十分 ✗ (二 → 两)
3. 三点五分 ✗ (missing 零 — should be 三点零五分)
4. 七点二十分 ✓ (correct)
5. 十二点一分 ✗ (missing 零 — 十二点零一分)
6. 五点三十分 ✓ (correct — also 五点半)

After each, choral: "Correct!" or "Wrong! Should be..."

- **Scaffold:** Word bank on projector.
- **Extension:** Why is each wrong? Explain in your own words.

##### Sprint 4: Partner Speed Round (5 min)
Pairs. Student A looks at clock images on projector (teacher controls pace — 8 seconds each). Student A says the Chinese time. Student B writes digits on paper. Swap after 6 clocks. Pairs count how many they got right. Loudest correct pair gets a point.

- **OTR:** Every pair active. Teacher cold calls 3 pairs for a demo answer.

##### Sprint 5: Pinyin Circle (Textbook p89) (5 min)
This directly implements the textbook p89 "Circle the correct pinyin" exercise. Projector shows pinyin pairs. Students write the letter (A or B) of the CORRECT pinyin on paper. Hold it up after each. Choral read the correct one together.

| # | Option A | Option B | Correct |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | shén | sén | A (shén — 什) |
| 2 | nǐ | ní | A (nǐ — 你, 3rd tone) |
| 3 | mèi | měi | A (mèi — 妹, 4th tone) |
| 4 | qī | chī | A (qī — 七) |
| 5 | zhè | zhé | B (zhè — 这, no second tone) |
| 6 | zài | zhài | A (zài — 在) |
| 7 | hào | hòu | A (hào — 号) |
| 8 | bàn | bìng | A (bàn — 半) |
| 9 | guó | guǒ | A (guó — 国, 2nd tone) |

Quick pace — 30 seconds each. "This is exactly the kind of pinyin precision you need for your test and for accurate pronunciation."

- **OTR:** Every student writes every answer — 9 decisions each.

#### Plenary (43–50 min)
- **Revisit Success Criteria:** Read aloud together. Self-check.
- **Self-assessment:** "Which sprint was hardest for you? Write the sprint number on your paper." Teacher collects to adjust Lesson 3 review.
- **Exit ticket:** "Write 8:07 in Chinese." (八点零七分 — testing 零)
- **Preview:** "Next lesson: the easiest pattern — half past!"
