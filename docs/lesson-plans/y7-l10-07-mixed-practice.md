## Lesson 7: Mixed Practice — Time Olympics

**Option: B — Time Olympics (4 competition rounds)**

### Learning Intention
We are learning to use all time expressions fluently and accurately under time pressure.

### Success Criteria
- I can hear Chinese time and write the correct digits (听中文时间写数字)
- I can see a clock and say the most natural Chinese expression instantly (看钟表秒说中文)
- I can switch between 点, 分, 半, 刻, and 差 without hesitation (灵活切换所有时间表达)

### Materials Needed
- Projector/TV
- A4 paper for each student
- Olympics score sheet (one per student — to track their own points)
- 4 round materials pre-loaded on projector
- Timer visible on projector

---

### Lesson Sequence

#### Review (0–8 min)
**What:** Quick warm-up covering all five patterns.

**How:** "Pattern sprint." Teacher projects 15 clock faces at 5-second intervals. Students write the DIGIT on paper (not Chinese, just numbers, to warm up speed). After the 15th, quick self-check: how many out of 15? Raise your hand if you got 15. 14. 13. Under 10 — those students need to focus most today.

**Connection:** "Today is not a normal lesson. It's the Time Olympics — four rounds of competition. You're not competing against each other. You're competing against yourself. Your goal: beat your own score each round."

#### Learning Intention & Success Criteria (8–10 min)
Display. "Olympics rules: each round has a different skill. Points add up. Bronze, silver, and gold medals at the end." Explain the 4-round structure briefly.

#### Round 1: Listening Relay (10 min)

**Skill:** Listen to Chinese → write correct digits.

Teacher says a time in Chinese (e.g. "差十分七点"). Students write the DIGITS on paper (6:50). Hold it up. Teacher reveals correct answer on projector. Self-mark: ✓ or ✗.

20 items. Each item = 5 points. Maximum round score: 100.

Item list (builds in difficulty):
1. 三点 (3:00) — easy warm-up
2. 六点半 (6:30)
3. 九点一刻 (9:15)
4. 十二点零五分 (12:05) — 零 trap
5. 差十分七点 (6:50) — first 差
6. 四点三刻 (4:45)
7. 两点二十分 (2:20)
8. 差五分八点 (7:55)
9. 七点零一分 (7:01) — tricky 零
10. 十一点半 (11:30)
11. 差一刻十二点 (11:45)
12. 五点十一分 (5:11)
13. 差二分六点 (5:58)
14. 三点零七分 (3:07)
15. 八点三刻 (8:45)
16. 差十分两点 (1:50) — 两!
17. 十二点半 (12:30)
18. 六点十九分 (6:19)
19. 差五分四点 (3:55)
20. 九点零九分 (9:09) — double 零 concept!

After all 20: students total their score. Write it on their Olympics sheet. Teacher asks: "Who got 100? 95? 90?" Quick celebration.

**OTR:** 20 responses per student.

#### Round 2: Speaking Sprint (8 min)

**Skill:** See clock → say Chinese (choose most natural pattern).

Projector shows 15 clock faces. Teacher points to each clock, counts down "3, 2, 1" — whole class chorally says the Chinese. Teacher immediately gives the correct answer. Students self-score: if what YOU said matched the correct answer, give yourself a point.

15 items. Each = 7 points (total 105, plus 5 bonus available — see below). Maximum: 110.

Sequence (weighted toward needing pattern choice):
1. 2:00 (两点 — 两!)
2. 3:30 (三点半)
3. 7:45 (七点三刻)
4. 9:15 (九点一刻)
5. 6:50 (差十分七点)
6. 12:05 (十二点零五分)
7. 4:00 (四点)
8. 8:55 (差五分九点)
9. 11:15 (十一点一刻)
10. 5:40 (差二十分六点 OR 五点四十分)
11. 1:30 (一点半)
12. 10:45 (十点三刻)
13. 3:10 (三点十分)
14. 2:50 (差十分三点)
15. 9:00 (九点)

**Bonus round (after the 15):** Teacher shows 2 more clocks. For #10 and #14, if anyone chose the 差 version (more natural), add 5 bonus points.

After all: students tally their Round 2 score. Add to Olympics sheet.

**OTR:** Choral — 15 speaking moments.

#### Round 3: Error Hunter (7 min)

**Skill:** Identify and correct mistakes.

Projector shows 12 Chinese time expressions. Some are correct, some contain errors. Students write ✓ or ✗ on paper. If ✗, write the CORRECT version. Self-mark after each.

12 items. Each correct judgment + correction = 10 points. Maximum: 120.

| Item | Correct? | Why wrong | Correct version |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. 二点 | ✗ | 二→两 before 点 | 两点 |
| 2. 三点一刻 | ✓ | — | — |
| 3. 三点五分 | ✗ | Missing 零 | 三点零五分 |
| 4. 差十分七点 | ✓ | — | — |
| 5. 七点三刻 | ✓ | — (7:45) | — |
| 6. 五点差十分 | ✗ | Word order: 差 first | 差十分五点 |
| 7. 两点半 | ✓ | — | — |
| 8. 十二点零一分 | ✓ | — | — |
| 9. 四点六十 | ✗ | No such time. Next hour | 五点 |
| 10. 差一刻十二点 | ✓ | — (11:45) | — |
| 11. 八点十刻 | ✗ | 刻 only for 1 or 3 | 八点... 得看具体时间 |
| 12. 差十分二点 | ✗ | 二→两 | 差十分两点 |

After each: choral "Correct!" or "Wrong! Should be..." Teacher explains briefly for items 5, 9, 11 (the conceptual ones).

**OTR:** 12 decisions per student + choral response after each.

#### Round 4: Champion's Choice (7 min)

**Skill:** Choose the MOST natural expression from multiple options.

Projector shows a clock + 2 Chinese expressions. Students write the letter (A or B) of the MORE NATURAL choice. Then reveal and discuss.

10 items. Each = 10 points. Maximum: 100.

| Clock | Option A | Option B | Best |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3:00 | 三点 | 三点零分 | A — shorter |
| 7:30 | 七点半 | 七点三十分 | A — more spoken |
| 6:45 | 六点三刻 | 六点四十五分 | A — more spoken |
| 2:50 | 两点五十分 | 差十分三点 | B — more spoken |
| 9:15 | 九点一刻 | 九点十五分 | A — more spoken |
| 11:55 | 十一点五十五分 | 差五分十二点 | B — significantly shorter |
| 1:05 | 一点零五分 | 差五十五分两点 | A — 差 version too long |
| 4:45 | 四点三刻 | 差一刻五点 | A — 三刻 is shortest |
| 8:50 | 差十分九点 | 八点五十分 | A — 差 is smoother |
| 12:30 | 十二点三十分 | 十二点半 | B — more spoken |

**Discussion after each:** Why is one better? (Shorter, more spoken, convention.)

#### Olympics Medal Ceremony (Remaining time in Plenary, ~4 min)

Students total their scores across all 4 rounds (max: 100 + 110 + 120 + 100 = 430 points).
- Gold: 380+ points (🥇)
- Silver: 300–379 points (🥈)
- Bronze: 200–299 points (🥉)

Standing ovation for gold medalists. Specific praise: "Who got perfect on the Error Hunter round? Who aced the Listening Relay?"

#### Plenary (43–50 min)
- **Revisit Success Criteria:** "After today's Olympics, how do you feel about telling time in Chinese?" Students write one sentence reflection on paper.
- **Self-assessment:** "Which round was your strongest? Which was hardest? This tells you what to review before the test."
- **Exit ticket:** "Write the ONE time pattern (点/分/半/刻/差) you want to practise more before the test."
- **Preview:** "Next lesson: final review and test prep — 'Beat the Test' game show!"
