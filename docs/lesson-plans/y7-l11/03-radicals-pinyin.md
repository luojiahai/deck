## Lesson 3: Radicals + Pinyin Listening

**Option: C — Game-dominated**

### Learning Intention
We are learning to recognise 6 new radicals and to hear the difference between an/en/in/un/ün sounds.

### Success Criteria
- I can name the 6 new radicals and give an example character for each (说出六个新部首和例字)
- I can spot a radical inside an unfamiliar character (在生字里找到部首)
- I can add the correct tonal mark after listening to a sound (听音写声调)

### Materials Needed
- Projector/TV (with audio for CD T51)
- Radical flashcards (large, one per radical, for Slap the Character) — 2 sets
- Radical Bingo cards (9-square, radicals + example characters mixed) — one per student
- Writing Relay board space (chalkboard/whiteboard at front) — 4 team lanes
- A4 paper (for pinyin listening task)

---

### Lesson Sequence

#### Review (0–8 min)

**What:** Time-of-day + clock-time conversions from Lesson 2.

**How:** Quick-fire round — teacher shows a digital time, first team to correctly call out the Chinese conversion (as a team huddle-and-shout) scores a point. 5 rounds: 07:15 / 12:30 / 18:45 / 09:00 / 15:50 (差 form).

**Connection:** "You've mastered time. Today we switch gears completely — we're going to look INSIDE characters at the building blocks that help you guess new words, and sharpen your ear for tricky vowel sounds."

#### Learning Intention & Success Criteria (8–10 min)
Project and read aloud. Students repeat each criterion once.

#### I Do (10–20 min)

##### New Radicals (p.81, Exercise 5)
| 部首 | Meaning | Example character |
|---|---|---|
| 穴 | cave | 穿 |
| 矢 | arrow | 短 |
| 页 | page | 颜 |
| 扌(才) | hand | 拉 |
| 艹 | grass | 英 |
| 走 | walk | 起 |

Model each radical on the projector: show the radical alone, then the example character with the radical highlighted in colour. Point out: "起 (get up) — from this unit's own vocabulary! — has the 走 (walk) radical, because getting up is the start of moving through your day."

##### CFU Checkpoints
- "Which radical means 'grass'?" Cold call. (艹)
- Teacher shows 颜 highlighted — "which radical is inside?" Students write on paper, hold up. (页)
- "起 has which radical?" (走)

##### Pinyin Focus (Ex6, p.81, CD T51)
Explain the 5 target finals: an, en, in, un, ün. Model the vowel-mouth-shape difference for each (an — wide open; en — neutral; in — smile; un — rounded; ün — rounded + smile). Play the CD track once through without pausing so students hear the natural pace, then explain the task: listen and add the correct tone mark above the vowel.

#### Flexible Practice (20–43 min)

##### Activity 1: Radical Slap (Slap the Character) (6 min)
Two large sets of the 6 radical cards spread on a table/board at the front. Two students at a time (one from each half of the class) race to slap the correct radical when the teacher calls its meaning or shows the example character. Winner stays up for the next round; rotate through the whole class in pairs.

- **Scaffold:** Radical table stays projected as a reference throughout.
- **Extension:** For advanced students, teacher calls the example character (e.g. "颜") instead of the meaning, requiring them to identify which radical it contains.
- **OTR:** Every student gets at least one turn at the front; the rest of the class chants the correct radical name after each slap.

##### Activity 2: Radical Bingo (7 min)
Each student has a 9-square bingo card with a mix of radicals and example characters. Teacher calls out a meaning (e.g. "arrow") or reads an example character aloud; students cross off the matching square. First to complete a line calls "Bingo!" and must read back their full line correctly to win.

- **Scaffold:** Struggling students may use the radical table as an open reference during play.
- **Extension:** Fast finishers who get Bingo early can keep playing for a full-card win, matching all 9 squares.
- **OTR:** Whole-class choral confirmation of the correct answer after every call.

##### Activity 3: Pinyin Listening — Add the Tone (CD T51) — You Do (7 min)
Students receive an A4 sheet with 10 syllables (untoned finals: an, en, in, un, ün mixed with different initials). Play the CD track pausing after each item; students write the tone mark in the correct place. Self-mark against a projected answer key at the end.

- **Scaffold:** Students who need more support get the track played twice per item and may check the vowel-mouth-shape chart from the I Do phase.
- **Extension:** Fluent students also write the full pinyin syllable with initial + final + tone, not just the tone mark.
- **OTR:** After each item, thumbs up/down check before moving to the next.

##### Game: Radical Writing Relay (5 min)
4 teams line up facing the board. Teacher calls a radical's meaning; the first student in each line runs up and writes ONE correct example character containing that radical (can reuse the 6 taught, or a bonus character if they know one), then tags the next teammate. Fastest correct team scores a point. 4 rounds, one per radical group taught today (穴/矢/页/扌, then repeat 艹/走 in round 5–6 if time allows).

- **Setup needed:** Board space for 4 lanes, chalk/markers ready per team.

#### Plenary (43–50 min)
- **Revisit Success Criteria:** Read aloud; students show 👍/😐/👎.
- **Self-assessment:** On paper: "Write the radical you find easiest to spot, and one you still need to practise."
- **Exit ticket:** "Which radical is inside 短? Which sound was trickiest today — an, en, in, un, or ün?" Collect on the way out.
- **Preview:** "Next lesson: brand new verbs for your daily routine — getting up, eating breakfast, going to school, and going to bed."
