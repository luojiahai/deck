## Lesson 2: Time-of-Day Conversion Practice

**Option: B — Writing-focused**

### Learning Intention
We are learning to combine a time-of-day word with a clock time to describe when something happens.

### Success Criteria
- I can convert a digital time into Chinese with the correct time-of-day prefix (用时间词+点说完整时间, e.g. 16:45 → 下午四点三刻)
- I can order a set of Chinese characters by stroke count (按笔画数排序)
- I can spot which time-of-day word is needed just from the digital time shown (根据数字时间判断该用哪个时间词)

### Materials Needed
- Projector/TV
- Written conversion worksheet (8 digital times, Ex3 format, p.80) — one per student
- Stroke-count ordering cards (Ex4, p.80) — one set per pair, plus an answer key for self-check
- A4 paper

---

### Lesson Sequence

#### Review (0–8 min)

**What:** Lesson 1's five time-of-day words and their ranges.

**How:** Teacher projects 6 digital times one at a time (e.g. 06:30, 11:00, 14:00, 19:00, 09:45, 12:30). Students write only the matching time-of-day word (not the full time) on paper, hold up. Cold call 2 students per item to justify their choice.

**Connection:** "Yesterday you learned to spot the right time-of-day word. Today you put the whole thing together — time-of-day word + the exact time — just like the textbook asks in Exercise 3."

#### Learning Intention & Success Criteria (8–10 min)
Project and read aloud. Students echo each criterion once.

#### I Do (10–20 min)

##### Key Pattern: Time-of-Day + Clock Time
Model the conversion process with a think-aloud: "16:45 — first, which time-of-day word? 16:00 is afternoon, so 下午. Then the clock time: 45 minutes is 三刻. Put them together: 下午四点三刻."

Worked examples on the projector:
| Digital | Chinese |
|---|---|
| 07:00 | 早上七点 |
| 10:30 | 上午十点半 |
| 12:00 | 中午十二点 |
| 16:45 | 下午四点三刻 |
| 20:15 | 晚上八点一刻 |

Insert one "silly" example: "我晚上五点起床" — wait for reactions, then correct: "晚上 is evening, not when most people get up!"

##### CFU Checkpoints
- "14:00 — which time-of-day word?" Write on paper, hold up. (下午)
- "What's wrong with 早上十二点?" — cold call (should be 中午 or specify — 12:00 noon is 中午, not 早上)
- "Convert 06:50 to Chinese using 差." Write and hold up. (差十分七点 + 早上, i.e. 早上差十分七点)

#### Flexible Practice (20–43 min)

##### Activity 1: Guided Conversion Worksheet (Ex3, p.80) — We Do (10 min)
Students work through the first 4 of 8 digital times on the worksheet as a class — teacher does #1 together on the board, then students attempt #2–4 individually and check with a partner before the teacher reveals answers.

- **Scaffold:** Time-of-day range chart (from Lesson 1) stays projected throughout; sentence frame ___ + 点 + (分/半/刻) printed at the top of the worksheet.
- **Extension:** Fluent students also write the 差 form where applicable (e.g. an item at :50 or :55).
- **OTR:** Turn-and-talk before each reveal — every student commits to an answer before hearing the correct one.

##### Activity 2: Independent Conversion — You Do (8 min)
Students complete the remaining 4 items on the worksheet independently, then self-mark against a projected answer key.

- **Scaffold:** Students who need support get a version with the time-of-day word already given, only needing to add the clock-time portion.
- **Extension:** Students write one additional time of their own choosing and convert it, then swap with a partner to check.
- **OTR:** Cold call 4 students to read out one answer each before self-marking begins.

##### Activity 3: Stroke-Count Ordering (Ex4, p.80) — You Do (5 min)
In pairs, students arrange the 12 character cards (from the lesson's vocabulary so far) in order of stroke count, lowest to highest, and write the order on paper.

- **Scaffold:** Pairs may count strokes together aloud, tracing each character with a finger first.
- **Extension:** Once ordered, students also group characters that share a stroke count and identify the shared count.
- **OTR:** Every pair must show their ordered list before checking the projected answer.

##### Game: Stroke-Count Relay (5 min)
Class splits into 4 teams. Each team receives a shuffled set of the same 12 character cards face-down at the front. One student per team runs up, picks a card, places it in the next open slot in an ordering line on the board/table, and runs back — next teammate goes. First team with a fully correct stroke-count order (checked together at the end) wins.

- **Setup needed:** 4 identical card sets, clear floor space to run between desks and the front board.

#### Plenary (43–50 min)
- **Revisit Success Criteria:** Read aloud; students show 👍/😐/👎 for each.
- **Self-assessment:** On paper: "Write one digital time and its Chinese conversion that you're confident about."
- **Exit ticket:** "Convert 15:30 to Chinese (time-of-day + clock time)." Collect on the way out. (下午三点半)
- **Preview:** "Next lesson: we learn 6 new radicals and practise listening for pinyin with an, en, in, un, ün sounds."
