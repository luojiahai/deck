## Lesson 4: Daily Routine Verbs + Text 2

**Option: B — Writing-focused**

### Learning Intention
We are learning to write the six daily-routine verbs and use them with a time to describe a day.

### Success Criteria
- I can write 起床/吃早饭/去上学/上课/放学/睡觉 (write the six routine actions)
- I can copy and complete Text 2's six routine sentences with the correct time (完成日常起居的六个句子)
- I can build my own sentence using a routine verb + a time (自己写一个日常起居的句子)

### Materials Needed
- Projector/TV (with audio for CD T52)
- Character writing practice sheet (grid paper, stroke order shown) for the 6 target verbs
- Text 2 cloze worksheet (6 sentences with the verb or time blanked out) — one per student
- A4 paper

---

### Lesson Sequence

#### Review (0–8 min)

**What:** The 6 radicals from Lesson 3 and time-of-day + clock-time conversion.

**How:** Teacher projects 3 characters containing this unit's radicals (e.g. 短, 颜, 起) — students write which radical is inside each, hold up. Then 3 quick time conversions (digital → Chinese) as a warm-up recall. Cold call to check.

**Connection:** "You now know radicals, times, and time-of-day words. Today we put it all together to describe an actual DAY — what you do, and when."

#### Learning Intention & Success Criteria (8–10 min)
Project and read aloud. Students echo each criterion once.

#### I Do (10–20 min)

##### New Vocabulary (p.82)
| 汉字 | 拼音 | English |
|---|---|---|
| 起 | qǐ | get up |
| 床 | chuáng | bed |
| 起床 | qǐ chuáng | get up |
| 吃 | chī | eat |
| 饭 | fàn | cooked rice; meal |
| 早饭 | zǎo fàn | breakfast |
| 去 | qù | go |
| 上学 | shàng xué | go to school; attend school |
| 课 | kè | class; period |
| 上课 | shàng kè | attend class |
| 放 | fàng | let go; release |
| 放学 | fàng xué | classes are over |
| 睡 | shuì | sleep |
| 觉 | jiào | sleep |
| 睡觉 | shuì jiào | sleep; go to bed |

Model pronunciation twice per word, class repeats twice. Point out 起床 uses the 走 radical taught last lesson (起).

##### Text 2 — Six Daily Routine Scenes (p.82, CD T52)
Play the audio once through, students follow along on the projector:

```
我早上六点半起床。
我六点三刻吃早饭。
我七点一刻去上学。
我们八点上课。
我们下午三点半放学。
我晚上九点半睡觉。
```

Read together chorally, line by line. Ask 2 comprehension questions: "What time does this person get up?" (六点半) / "What time do classes finish?" (三点半 / 下午三点半).

##### CFU Checkpoints
- "How do you say 'eat breakfast'?" Write on paper, hold up. (吃早饭)
- "What's the difference between 上学 and 上课?" — cold call (上学 = go to/attend school generally; 上课 = attend a specific class/period)
- "Which verb pairs with 觉?" (睡 → 睡觉)

#### Flexible Practice (20–43 min)

##### Activity 1: Character Writing Practice — We Do (8 min)
Students use the grid-paper worksheet to trace then copy each of the 6 target verbs (起床/吃早饭/去上学/上课/放学/睡觉) 3 times each, following the stroke order shown on the projector.

- **Scaffold:** Pinyin printed above each character on the worksheet; teacher models stroke order on the board first for the trickiest character (觉).
- **Extension:** Fluent students also write the pinyin and English meaning from memory next to each character, without looking at the word bank.
- **OTR:** Turn-and-talk — partners check each other's stroke order and character shape before moving to the next character.

##### Activity 2: Text 2 Cloze Completion — You Do (8 min)
Students complete the cloze worksheet: Text 2's six sentences with either the verb or the time blanked out (mixed, so students can't just guess a pattern). They fill in the blanks from memory, then self-check against the projected original.

- **Scaffold:** Word bank of the 6 verbs and a time-of-day chart available for students who need it.
- **Extension:** Students who finish early rewrite all 6 sentences describing their OWN daily routine, changing the times and adding a time-of-day word.
- **OTR:** Cold call 6 students, one per sentence, to read their completed sentence aloud.

##### Activity 3: Build-Your-Own Sentence — You Do (5 min)
Individually, students write one new sentence (not from Text 2) combining a time-of-day word + clock time + one of today's routine verbs, e.g. 我下午四点半吃早饭 (a "silly" one is fine for fun) or a realistic one about themselves.

- **Scaffold:** Sentence frame on the projector: 我 + [time-of-day] + [time] + [verb phrase]。
- **Extension:** Students add a second clause using 我们 for a shared routine, e.g. 我们八点上课。
- **OTR:** Pair swap — partner reads the sentence aloud and gives a thumbs up/down on whether it makes sense.

##### Game: Sequence the Scenes (5 min)
Teacher hands out 6 strips (one per team of 4–5), each with one of Text 2's sentences written but shuffled out of chronological order. Teams race to lay the strips down on their desk in the correct time order (起床 → 吃早饭 → 去上学 → 上课 → 放学 → 睡觉). First correct team wins.

- **Setup needed:** 6 sentence strips per team, printed in advance; enough desk space to lay them out.

#### Plenary (43–50 min)
- **Revisit Success Criteria:** Read aloud; students show 👍/😐/👎.
- **Self-assessment:** On paper: "Write the routine verb you're most confident writing, and one you still need practice with."
- **Exit ticket:** "Write one sentence: what time do YOU get up? (Use 起床.)" Collect on the way out.
- **Preview:** "Next lesson: we turn these sentences into questions, so you can ask a classmate about their routine, not just describe your own."
