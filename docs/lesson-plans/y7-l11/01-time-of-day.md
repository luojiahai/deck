## Lesson 1: Time-of-Day Vocabulary + Greetings

**Option: B — Writing-focused**

### Learning Intention
We are learning to write and use the five parts-of-day words in Chinese.

### Success Criteria
- I can write 早上/上午/中午/下午/晚上 (write the five time-of-day words)
- I can match each time-of-day word to the correct time range (匹配时间词和时间范围)
- I can complete a greeting that matches the correct time of day (根据时间写问候语)

### Materials Needed
- Projector/TV
- Printed sun-arc diagram worksheet (5 blank labels matching Text 1, p.78) — one per student
- Character copying worksheet for the 9 new words
- Dictionary race cards (phrases with pinyin: 校车, 火车, 出租车, 地铁, etc. — pulled forward from Lesson 12 vocab for the race, meanings hidden) — one set per group of 3–4, plus classroom/pocket dictionaries or word lists
- A4 paper (for written responses)

---

### Lesson Sequence

#### Review (0–8 min)

**What:** Lesson 10 clock-telling skills (o'clock, half, quarter, 差).

**How:** Teacher projects 6 clock faces one at a time (mix of exact/half/quarter/差 times). Students write the Chinese on paper, hold up after each. Cold call 2 students per item to say it aloud.

1. 3:00 (三点) 2. 7:30 (七点半) 3. 9:15 (九点一刻) 4. 2:45 (两点三刻) 5. 差十分七点 (6:50, shown as digits — students write using 差) 6. 12:00 (十二点)

**Connection:** "You can tell the time perfectly now. But time on its own doesn't tell a story — today we learn the words for WHEN in the day something happens: morning, noon, evening."

#### Learning Intention & Success Criteria (8–10 min)
Project and read aloud. Students repeat each criterion once chorally. Keep visible all lesson.

#### I Do (10–20 min)

##### New Vocabulary (p.79)
| 汉字 | 拼音 | English |
|---|---|---|
| 早 | zǎo | early; morning |
| 早上 | zǎo shàng | (early) morning |
| 午 | wǔ | noon |
| 上午 | shàng wǔ | before noon; morning |
| 中午 | zhōng wǔ | noon |
| 下午 | xià wǔ | afternoon |
| 晚上 | wǎn shàng | (in the) evening |
| 下 | xià | down; get off |
| 晚 | wǎn | evening; late |

Model pronunciation twice per word, class repeats twice (choral).

##### Text 1 — The Sun-Arc Diagram (p.78, CD T45)
Project the sun-arc diagram with the five time bands and model each with its scene:

| Term | Time range | Scene |
|---|---|---|
| 早上 | 5:00–8:00 | 早上七点 (going to school) |
| 上午 | before noon | 上午十点 (studying) |
| 中午 | noon | 中午十二点 (eating) |
| 下午 | afternoon | 下午四点 (playing football) |
| 晚上 | 18:00–24:00 | 晚上八点 (watching TV) |

**Critical rule:** 早上 (5:00–8:00) and 上午 (before noon, i.e. up to 12:00) both translate as "morning" in English but cover different ranges — 早上 is the early part only. Write both on the board with their time ranges underlined.

##### CFU Checkpoints
- "早上七点 — is this 早上 or 上午? Fingers: 1 = 早上, 2 = 上午." (1 — 早上)
- "十点半 — 早上 or 上午? Fingers." (2 — 上午, since it's after 8:00 but before noon)
- Teacher says "下午四点". Students write which time-of-day word on paper, hold up. (下午)
- "What's the difference between 早上 and 上午?" — cold call one student to explain in English.

#### Flexible Practice (20–43 min)

##### Activity 1: Sun-Arc Diagram Labelling — We Do (8 min)
Students receive the printed sun-arc worksheet (blank labels). Teacher re-projects Text 1's diagram at half-speed; students copy the correct Chinese word into each of the 5 blanks, then copy one matching scene sentence under each (e.g. 早上七点 — 去上学).

- **Scaffold:** Word bank of all 5 terms + pinyin printed at the bottom of the worksheet.
- **Extension:** Fluent students also write the time range in Chinese numerals next to each label (e.g. 早上 5:00–8:00 → 早上五点到八点).
- **OTR:** Turn-and-talk — check answers with a partner before teacher reveals, every student compares every label.

##### Activity 2: Greeting Completion (Ex1, p.79) — You Do (8 min)
Project the 4 greeting scenes from Ex1 (people meeting at different times of day). Students write the matching greeting for each scene on paper, e.g. scene showing 早上 → 早上好！ Then in pairs, one reads their written greeting aloud, partner identifies which time of day it matches.

- **Scaffold:** Sentence frame on projector: ___好！ (fill with 早上/中午/下午/晚上). Word bank visible.
- **Extension:** Students add a second line responding appropriately, e.g. A: 早上好！ B: 早上好！你要去哪儿？(preview — accept English answer if needed).
- **OTR:** Cold call 4 pairs to perform their written greeting aloud.

##### Activity 3: Dictionary Race (Ex2, p.79) — You Do (5 min)
Groups of 3–4 receive a set of phrase cards with pinyin only (phrases pulled forward from Lesson 12: 校车, 火车, 出租车, 地铁, etc.). Groups look up each phrase and write the correct meaning on paper. Fastest group with all correct meanings wins.

- **Scaffold:** Groups with weaker readers get fewer cards (4 instead of 6) and may work in a threesome with a stronger reader.
- **Extension:** Fast-finishing groups write one full sentence using a found phrase (e.g. 我坐地铁上学).
- **OTR:** Every student in the group must write at least one meaning in their own hand before the group can submit.

##### Game: Character Bingo (5 min)
Printed 9-square bingo cards with the 9 new words (mixed order per card). Teacher calls out pinyin or English; students cross off the matching character. First to complete a line calls "Bingo!" and reads their line aloud to confirm.

- **Setup needed:** Printed bingo cards (varied layouts), no devices required.

#### Plenary (43–50 min)
- **Revisit Success Criteria:** Teacher reads each criterion; students show 👍/😐/👎.
- **Self-assessment:** On paper: "Write the time-of-day word you find easiest, and one you still mix up."
- **Exit ticket:** "Write the Chinese for 'morning' (5:00–8:00) and 'morning' (before noon) — label which is which." Collect on the way out; scan for 早上/上午 confusion.
- **Preview:** "Next lesson: we take a digital clock time and add the correct time-of-day word in front of it — like 下午四点三刻."
