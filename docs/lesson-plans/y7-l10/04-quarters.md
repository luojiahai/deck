## Lesson 4: Quarters (刻)

**Option: B — Clock face heavy**

### Learning Intention
We are learning to say quarter-past and quarter-to times using 刻.

### Success Criteria
- I can say quarter-past times using 一刻 (用一刻说X点一刻)
- I can say quarter-to times using 三刻 (用三刻说X点三刻)
- I can choose the right pattern — 点, 分, 半, or 刻 — for any clock I see (看钟选择正确的表达)

### Materials Needed
- Projector/TV
- A4 paper
- 20 clock face images for rapid flashing (pre-loaded: mix of :00, :05, :15, :30, :45, :50)
- Printed worksheet: 15 clock faces → write Chinese
- Slap the Board: 12 Chinese time expressions pre-written on board

---

### Lesson Sequence

#### Review (0–8 min)
**What:** 点, 分, 半 — all three previous patterns. Quick warm-up.

**How:** Projector flashes 10 clock faces. Students write the digit time on paper for each (not Chinese — just digits, to save time). Teacher says "Hold it up!" after each. Gauge accuracy, note any widespread errors.

Times: 3:00, 7:20, 2:30, 9:05, 12:00, 5:15, 10:45, 6:30, 8:07, 11:50. The :15 and :45 times are previews — students may not know how to say them yet, and that's fine.

**Connection:** "You got :15 and :45 as digits. Now let's learn how to SAY them in Chinese. Today: 刻 — the quarter pattern."

#### Learning Intention & Success Criteria (8–10 min)
Display. Read aloud. "By the end of today, you'll be able to say :15 and :45 without thinking."

#### I Do (10–20 min)

##### New Vocabulary
| 汉字 | 拼音 | English |
|---|---|---|
| 刻 | kè | quarter (of an hour) |

##### Radical Spotlight (Textbook p85): 刂 and 米

Continuing the textbook's 6 radicals. Today we learn the 2 most directly relevant to time:

| 部首 | Name | Meaning | Role in this lesson |
|---|---|---|---|
| 刂 | dāo (立刀旁) | knife | **刻** contains 刂 on the right side! 亥 + 刂 = 刻 (a knife cutting time into quarters) |
| 米 | mǐ (米字旁) | rice | Seen in compounds. Also: 米 is a measure word for metres — appears in context of distance (coming in Lesson 12) |

Projector: show 刻 = 亥 + 刂. "See the knife radical on the right? That's 刂. When you see 刂 in a character, it's often about cutting or carving. Here: cutting an hour into quarters."

Students copy 刂 and 米 into notebooks. 30 seconds each.

##### Key Patterns

**Quarter past: X点一刻 = X:15**
Projector shows 3 clocks in sequence:
- 三点一刻 (3:15)
- 七点一刻 (7:15)
- 十二点一刻 (12:15)

Each: show clock → show characters → choral repeat × 2.

**Quarter to: X点三刻 = X:45**
Projector shows 3 clocks:
- 四点三刻 (4:45)
- 九点三刻 (9:45)
- 六点三刻 (6:45)

**Critical explanation:** 
- 一刻 = 1 quarter = 15 minutes past the hour
- 三刻 = 3 quarters = 45 minutes past the hour
- 四点三刻 = "4 o'clock + 3 quarters" = 4:45

**Visual aid on projector:**
```
4:00 ──→ 4:15 (一刻) ──→ 4:30 (半) ──→ 4:45 (三刻) ──→ 5:00
```

Clarify: 三刻 does NOT mean 3:03 or 3:15. It means "three quarters past the hour". This is different from English "quarter to" logic. The Chinese way: always count forward from the current hour.

**Alternative:** X点三刻 = X点四十五分 (both correct, 三刻 is more spoken).

##### 对比 on projector:

| Clock | Pattern | Chinese |
|---|---|---|
| 6:15 | 刻 (一刻) | 六点一刻 |
| 6:45 | 刻 (三刻) | 六点三刻 |
| 3:15 | 刻 | 三点一刻 |
| 3:45 | 刻 | 三点三刻 |

##### CFU Checkpoints
- "五点一刻 — is this 5:15 or 5:45?" (5:15 — thumbs up for 5:15, down for 5:45)
- "八点三刻 — is this 8:45 or 8:03?" (8:45 — write the digit on paper, hold up)
- "How else can you say 十点三刻?" (十点四十五分 — cold call)

#### Flexible Practice (20–43 min)

##### Activity 1: Clock Face Flood — We Do (10 min)
Projector shows a rapid sequence of 25 clock faces. Students chorally respond with the Chinese time. The sequence is designed:

- Block 1 (8 clocks): :15 times only — build 一刻 confidence
- Block 2 (8 clocks): :45 times only — build 三刻 confidence
- Block 3 (9 clocks): mixed — all four patterns (点, 分, 半, 刻) randomly shuffled

Pace: start at 5 seconds per clock, accelerate to 3 seconds. The :45 block starts slower (5 sec) then speeds up. For the mixed block, students must decide on the spot: "Which pattern do I use?"

- **Scaffold:** During Block 1–2: pattern formula on projector. During Block 3: formula removed.
- **Extension:** Fluent students stand up during Block 3 and add 早上/下午/晚上 before each answer.
- **OTR:** Every student responds to 25 clocks — massive choral OTR.

##### Activity 1b: Listen and Draw (Textbook p88) (3 min — embedded in Clock Face Flood)
This directly implements the textbook p88 "Listen and put hands on the clocks." Teacher pauses the projector after 5 random clocks from the flood. Teacher says the time in Chinese ONE MORE TIME (students don't see the clock — projector is frozen or blank). Students draw the clock hands on paper. Hold it up. Teacher reveals the correct clock.

- **OTR:** 5 listen-and-draw responses per student. Tests listening separately from reading.

##### Activity 2: Clock → Chinese Worksheet — You Do (7 min)
Worksheet with 15 clock faces. Students write the most natural Chinese expression for each. The 15 clocks are weighted toward :15 and :45, with some review of :00, :05, :30 mixed in. Partner check after completion. Teacher circulates, focusing on students who struggled during Block 3.

- **Scaffold:** Pattern reference on projector. Sentence starters: 现在 _____
- **Extension:** For each clock, write BOTH versions where applicable (e.g. 四点三刻 AND 四点四十五分).

##### Game: Slap the Board — Quarters Edition (6 min)
12 Chinese time expressions pre-written across the board, including:
六点一刻, 九点三刻, 三点十五分, 八点四十五分, 十二点半, 两点零五分, 五点, 七点二十分, 一点一刻, 四点三刻, 十一点, 差十分十点 (preview!)

Two students at the board, each with a fly swatter (or rolled-up paper). Teacher calls a time in English or shows a clock on projector. First to slap the CORRECT Chinese time wins and stays. Winner streak tracked on the board.

- **Setup needed:** Board pre-written. 2 fly swatters. Clock images pre-loaded.

#### Plenary (43–50 min)
- **Revisit Success Criteria:** Read aloud. Students self-rate: 👍/😐/👎 for each criterion.
- **Self-assessment:** "Quick show of hands: who finds 一刻 easy? Who finds 三刻 easy? Who finds both easy? Who is still confused about 三刻?" Adjust Lesson 5 review based on response.
- **Exit ticket:** "Write 10:45 in Chinese — use the 刻 pattern." (十点三刻)
- **Preview:** "Next lesson: you'll learn the 差 pattern — a completely different way to tell time. It's the last piece of the puzzle."
