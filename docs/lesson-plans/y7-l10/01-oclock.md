## Lesson 1: Numbers Review + O'clock (点)

**Option: A — Speaking + Listening heavy**

### Learning Intention
We are learning to say o'clock times in Chinese.

### Success Criteria
- I can recognise and say numbers 1–12 in Chinese (认识中文数字1–12)
- I can say o'clock times using 点 (用点说整点时间)
- I can tell the difference between 两 and 二 when telling time (区分两和二)

### Materials Needed
- Projector/TV with clock face images
- Printed number cards 1–12 (Chinese characters one side, digits other side) — one set per pair
- A4 paper (one per student for written responses)

---

### Lesson Sequence

#### Review (0–10 min) — Extended to cover textbook p84 + p87

**Part A: Previous Unit Questions (p84) — 5 min**

The textbook p84 has a "Group work: Try to complete the following questions" section reviewing Units 1–3 content. Teacher projects these question prompts one at a time. Students write a quick answer on paper (just keywords, not full sentences — speed focus):

1. 你叫什么名字？(Write your Chinese name)
2. 你是哪国人？(Write: 美国人/英国人/澳大利亚人/etc.)
3. 你家有几口人？(Write a number)
4. 你的生日是几月几号？(Write the date in digits)
5. 你住在哪儿？(Write the city)
6. 你今年上几年级？(Write 七年级)
7. 你家的电话号码是多少？(Write any fake number — just practise digits)

After each, cold call 2 students to say their answer aloud. Fast pace — 30 seconds per question.

**Part B: Math Sprint (p87) — 3 min**

The textbook p87 has a multiplication activity. Teacher says two single-digit numbers in Chinese (e.g. "七乘九"). Students calculate and write the product in Chinese characters on paper. Hold it up. Fast, fun, activates number vocabulary.

- 三 × 四 = ? (十二)
- 五 × 六 = ? (三十)
- 二 × 九 = ? (十八)
- 七 × 七 = ? (四十九)

**Connection:** "You just proved you remember your numbers. Today we're using them to tell the time — starting with o'clock."

#### Learning Intention & Success Criteria (10–12 min)
Display on projector. Read aloud, students repeat. Keep visible all lesson.

#### I Do (12–20 min)

##### New Vocabulary
| 汉字 | 拼音 | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 点 | diǎn | o'clock | |

##### Key Pattern: X + 点 = X o'clock

Model with projector — clock images paired with Chinese:
- 一点 (1:00), 三点 (3:00), 八点 (8:00), 十二点 (12:00)

**Critical rule:** For 2:00 → **两点** NOT 二点. Write a big ❌ over "二点". Choral drill: "两点!" (×5, loud). Cold call random students: "How do you say 2 o'clock?" every 2 minutes for the rest of the lesson to drill it in.

##### CFU Checkpoints (use finger voting — 1 finger = yes/agree, 2 fingers = no/disagree)
- "一点 is 1 o'clock — 1 finger yes, 2 fingers no." (1 finger)
- "二点 is correct — 1 finger yes, 2 fingers no." (2 fingers — cold call one student to explain why)
- "三点的拼音是 sān diǎn — fingers?" (1 finger)
- Teacher says "九点". Students write the DIGIT on paper, hold it up.

#### Flexible Practice (20–43 min)

##### Activity 1: Choral Clock Flash — We Do (8 min)
Projector shows clock faces (only o'clock times: 1:00, 3:00, 5:00, 7:00, 9:00, 11:00, 2:00, 4:00, 6:00, 8:00, 10:00, 12:00). Whole class chorally says the time. Start slow, speed up. Insert 2:00 twice — pause and emphasise 两点 each time.

- **Scaffold:** Number + 点 formula visible on projector throughout.
- **Extension:** Fluent students add 早上 or 下午 (preview of Lesson 11 vocab). Teacher: "Emma, say 7:00 but tell me if it's morning." → 早上七点.
- **OTR:** Choral response — every student speaks every time.

##### Activity 2: Clock Hands Challenge (Textbook p85) — You Do (8 min)
This directly implements the textbook p85 clock activity. Each student has A4 paper. Teacher says a time in Chinese (o'clock only today: 三点, 八点, 十二点, 六点, 两点). Students DRAW the clock face with hands positioned correctly. Hold it up after each. Teacher scans.

Then swap: Teacher PROJECTS a clock face on screen. Students write the Chinese on paper. Hold up.

- **Scaffold:** Number + 点 formula on projector throughout.
- **Extension:** Fluent students draw AND write the Chinese time on the same paper, then add 早上/下午.
- **OTR:** Every student draws/writes every time — 10+ responses.

##### Game: Telephone / 传声筒 (5 min)
Class in 4 rows (teams). Teacher whispers a time in Chinese to the first student in each row (e.g. "十一点"). Each student whispers it to the next. Last student runs to the board and writes the time in Chinese characters. First correct team wins a point.

Round 1: 五点, Round 2: 两点 (test!), Round 3: 十二点, Round 4: 八点. After each round, everyone says the correct answer together.

- **Setup needed:** Board space for 4 students to write simultaneously. Start times pre-planned.

#### Plenary (43–50 min)
- **Revisit Success Criteria:** Teacher reads each criterion aloud. Students show 1 finger (achieved), 2 fingers (getting there), fist (need help).
- **Self-assessment:** "On your paper, write the one time you feel most confident saying, and one time you still find tricky." Teacher collects papers (quick scan for 二点 errors).
- **Exit ticket:** Teacher says "2 o'clock". Students write the Chinese on their paper, hand it to teacher on the way out. Scan for 两点 vs 二点 — this is your #1 diagnostic.
- **Preview:** "Next lesson: we add minutes. You'll be able to say times like 3:10 and 7:25."
