# Lesson 8.3: Pinyin Focus (ou/uo) + Text 1 — Gao Li's Sporty Family

**Year Level:** Year 8
**Unit:** Unit 3 — 爱好 (Hobbies)
**Lesson:** Lesson 8 — Hobby (2): Sports 运动 | Lesson 3 of 8
**Duration:** 50 minutes

## Learning Intention
We are learning to distinguish the ou/uo pinyin sounds by ear and understand a spoken/written passage about a family's sports habits.

## Success Criteria
- I can hear the difference between ou and uo sounds and write the correct tonal mark
- I can read and understand Text 1 (高力's family) and answer comprehension questions
- I can say how long someone does a sport using verb + duration + object (跑一个小时步)

## Materials Needed
- Projector (Text 1 slide, listening grid)
- Mini whiteboards + markers
- Audio: CD Track 36 (Text 1), CD Track 37 (Act. 3 pinyin), CD Track 38 (Act. 5 listening grid) — or teacher-voiced equivalents if audio unavailable
- Printed listening grid (Act. 5: 6 students × 8 activities)

---

## Lesson Sequence

### Review (0–8 min)
**What:** Lessons 1–2 sports vocabulary and the 打/踢/no-verb rule — all 11 sports taught so far, 跟(和)……一起
**How:** Slap the Board — two students at a time, teacher says a sport in Chinese, students slap the matching English/picture card.
**Connection:** "You can already name 11 sports. Today you'll hear them used in a real family story, and train your ear on two tricky vowel sounds — ou and uo."

### Learning Intention & Success Criteria (8–10 min)
Display on projector. Say one word each with ou (谅口/走) and uo (说/多) so students hear the contrast before being told the learning intention — then reveal today's focus is telling those two sounds apart.

### I Do (10–20 min)
#### Vocabulary Note (from the text)
| 汉字 | 拼音 | English |
|---|---|---|
| 一家人 | yìjiā rén | the whole family |

This is the only new word in today's text — everything else (跑步, 游泳, 打网球, 每天, 早上) is review from Lessons 1–2 and earlier units.

Teach the pinyin distinction directly: **ou** is a smooth "oh-oo" glide (as in 走, 都, 后); **uo** starts with a rounded "w" onset (as in 说, 多, 我). Model both sounds several times, class repeats chorally, exaggerating the lip shape difference.

#### Key Sentence Pattern — Duration with Separable Verbs
> Verb + duration + object (no 的 needed for these sports)
> 他每天跑一个小时步。(NOT 他每天跑步一个小时 or 他每天跑一个小时的步)
> 她每天游半个小时泳。

Think-aloud: "跑步 and 游泳 are actually two-part words — verb + object glued together. When I add a duration, it slides in between the two parts, and unlike some other duration sentences you've seen, these usually drop 的." Contrast with 打篮球 → 他每天打一个小时篮球 (same pattern — duration between 打 and 篮球).

#### Text 1 (p. 70, CD Track 36)
> 我叫高力。我们一家人都喜欢运动。我爸爸喜欢跑步。他每天跑一个小时步。我妈妈喜欢游泳。她每天早上游泳。我呢，我喜欢跟朋友一起打网球。

Play the audio (or read aloud) once through, students follow along on the projected text. Read together chorally. Then ask:
1. Does everyone in Gao Li's family like sport? (是，一家人都喜欢运动)
2. What does dad like, and for how long each day? (跑步，一个小时)
3. What does mum like, and when? (游泳，每天早上)
4. What does Gao Li himself like, and with whom? (打网球，跟朋友一起)

#### CFU Checkpoints
- "Which word has the uo sound — 走 or 说? Thumbs up for 说."
- "Fill the gap: 他每天跑____步。(one hour)" — write on whiteboard (一个小时)
- "True or false: Gao Li's mum plays tennis." (false — she swims)

### Flexible Practice (20–43 min)

#### Activity 1: Pinyin Listening — ou/uo Tonal Marks (Act. 3, CD T37) — We Do (5 min)
Teacher plays/reads a list of words containing ou or uo; students write the pinyin with the correct tone mark on mini whiteboards, holding up after each one.
- **Scaffold:** A visual reminder card stays projected showing mouth-shape diagrams for ou vs uo.
- **Extension:** Students also identify whether the word is one they already know (e.g. 都, 说) and give its meaning.
- **OTR:** Whole-class whiteboard hold-up after every word.

#### Activity 2: Text 1 Choral Read + Comprehension — We Do (6 min)
Class reads Text 1 chorally twice (once slow, once at natural pace), then answers the 4 comprehension questions above as a whole group, cold-calling different students for each.
- **Scaffold:** English translations available to glance at for students who need it; sentence starters given for answers (他每天……).
- **Extension:** Fluent speakers restate the answer as a full Chinese sentence, not a fragment.
- **OTR:** Cold call spread across at least 6 different students.

#### Activity 3: Oral Duration Chain Drill (Act. 4) — You Do (4 min)
Teacher says a duration phrase (e.g. "five days", "three months"), first student translates aloud, next student says a new duration phrase for the next student to translate, continuing round the room.
- **Scaffold:** A projected reference list of duration words (天, 星期, 月, 小时, 分钟) stays visible.
- **Extension:** Students combine their duration with a sport: 他每天跑三个小时步。
- **OTR:** Every student in the chain must respond.

#### Activity 4: Listening Grid (Act. 5, CD T38) — You Do (6 min)
Students listen to 6 short descriptions of different people's sport habits and tick the correct box in an 8-activity grid for each person.
- **Scaffold:** Grid pre-labelled with pictures alongside the Chinese activity names.
- **Extension:** After ticking, students write one full sentence about one of the 6 people from memory.
- **OTR:** Independent listening task, checked as a class immediately after — students self-mark and raise hands for any they got wrong.

#### Game: Listen & Judge — True or False (2 min)
Teacher says 4–5 sentences about Text 1, some true and some deliberately false (e.g. "高力的爸爸喜欢游泳" — false). Students show thumbs up (true) or thumbs down (false) instantly.
- **Setup needed:** None — purely oral, works as a fast plenary bridge.

### Plenary (43–50 min)
- **Revisit Success Criteria:** Teacher reads each criterion; class gives an example (a word with ou, a fact from the text, a duration sentence).
- **Self-assessment:** Mini whiteboards — students write 👍/😐/👎 next to each criterion number.
- **Exit ticket:** "Write one sentence about how long you do a sport each day, using verb + duration + object."
- **Preview:** "Next lesson we'll practise asking and answering about sport duration with a partner, and act out what people are doing."
