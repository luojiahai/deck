# Lesson 8.4: Asking About Sport Duration + 正在 Practice

**Year Level:** Year 8
**Unit:** Unit 3 — 爱好 (Hobbies)
**Lesson:** Lesson 8 — Hobby (2): Sports 运动 | Lesson 4 of 8
**Duration:** 50 minutes

## Learning Intention
We are learning to ask and answer how long someone does a sport, and to describe what people are doing right now.

## Success Criteria
- I can ask 你每天/每个星期打几个小时+sport？ and answer with a duration
- I can write a full verb + duration + object sentence for any sport learned so far
- I can describe what someone is doing using 正在 + sport

## Materials Needed
- Projector (Act. 10's 7 picture slides)
- Mini whiteboards + markers
- Printed dialogue-writing frames (Act. 7)

---

## Lesson Sequence

### Review (0–8 min)
**What:** Lesson 3's duration pattern (verb + duration + object) and Text 1 facts about Gao Li's family
**How:** Mini whiteboards — teacher gives a sport + duration (e.g. 游泳/半个小时), students write the full sentence (她每天游半个小时泳), hold up.
**Connection:** "Yesterday you could make a duration statement. Today you'll learn to ask someone else how long they do a sport — and answer for yourself."

### Learning Intention & Success Criteria (8–10 min)
Display on projector. Ask a student directly in Chinese: "你每天打几个小时网球？" — let them attempt or guess, then reveal this is exactly today's target question.

### I Do (10–20 min)
#### Vocabulary Note
| 汉字 | 拼音 | English |
|---|---|---|
| 几个小时 | jǐ ge xiǎoshí | how many hours |

几 (how many) is review from earlier units — today it combines with 小时 to ask about duration specifically.

#### Key Sentence Pattern
> Question: 你每天/每个星期 + verb + 几个小时 + object？
> Answer: 我每天/每个星期 + verb + duration + object。
> 你每天跑几个小时步？我每天跑一个小时步。
> 你每个星期打几个小时网球？我每个星期打三个小时网球。

Model 3 examples with different sports and different time frames (每天 vs 每个星期). Also recap 正在 + verb for "in progress": 他正在打篮球。/ 她正在跑步。 Silly example: 你正在打乒乓球吗？不，我正在写作业！(pause for reactions).

#### CFU Checkpoints
- "Quick translate: 'How many hours do you swim every day?' Write it." (check: 你每天游几个小时泳？)
- "Error correction: 你每天几个小时打网球？What's wrong with the word order?" (check: 几个小时 comes after the verb, not before — 你每天打几个小时网球？)
- "Make a sentence with 正在 about someone playing basketball. Write it." (check: 他/她正在打篮球)

### Flexible Practice (20–43 min)

#### Activity 1: Written Duration Q&A Building (Act. 7) — We Do (8 min)
Teacher gives a sport + duration prompt (e.g. 网球/两个小时). Students write the full question-and-answer pair on their whiteboards: 你每天打几个小时网球？我每天打两个小时网球。 Repeat with 3 more prompts covering different verb types (打/踢/no-verb).
- **Scaffold:** Sentence frame with blanks projected throughout: 你每天/每个星期___几个小时___？我每天/每个星期___[duration]___。
- **Extension:** Students swap 每天 for 每个星期 and adjust the duration accordingly without being told to.
- **OTR:** Whole-class whiteboard hold-up after each prompt.

#### Activity 2: 正在 Picture Sentences (Act. 1 + Act. 10) — We Do → You Do (8 min)
Teacher shows each people-doing-sport picture one at a time — combine Act. 1's 5 pictures (p. 71) with Act. 10's 7 pictures (p. 76) into one set, or use whichever set is on hand. Students first write a 正在 sentence describing the picture on their whiteboard; once every picture has been written, teacher calls out phrases at random and students act out (TPR) the matching picture's action.
- **Scaffold:** Today's sport vocabulary list stays projected alongside the pictures.
- **Extension:** Students add a duration or 跟……一起 detail to their 正在 sentence if they can recall it, even though it's not required for 正在 statements.
- **OTR:** Every student writes a sentence for every picture; TPR round calls on the whole class simultaneously.

#### Activity 3: Written Partner Dialogue (Act. 7, extended) — You Do (7 min)
In pairs, students write a 3–4 exchange dialogue asking and answering about sport duration for two different sports, then swap papers with their partner to check for errors before reading it aloud together.
- **Scaffold:** A model dialogue stays projected as a reference structure to adapt.
- **Extension:** Students must use one 打-sport, one 踢/no-verb sport, and vary the time frame (每天 vs 每个星期) across their dialogue.
- **OTR:** Partner peer-check counts as a response for every student; 2–3 pairs read their dialogue to the class.

#### Game: Sentence Jumble (4 min)
Teacher projects a duration sentence with the words scrambled (e.g. 小时 / 每天 / 三个 / 我 / 打 / 篮球). Students race in pairs to write the words in correct order on their whiteboards.
- **Setup needed:** 3–4 scrambled sentences prepared in advance, projected one at a time.

### Plenary (43–50 min)
- **Revisit Success Criteria:** Choral read; class answers a live duration question from the teacher to demonstrate each criterion.
- **Self-assessment:** Thumbs up / sideways / down against each criterion.
- **Exit ticket:** "Write one question asking a friend how many hours they do a sport each week, and answer it yourself."
- **Preview:** "Next lesson we move to a new topic within sports — talking about TV, movies, and weekends, and the phrase 不太喜欢."
