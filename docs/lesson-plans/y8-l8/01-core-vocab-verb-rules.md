# Lesson 8.1: Core Sports Vocabulary + Verb Rules

**Year Level:** Year 8
**Unit:** Unit 3 — 爱好 (Hobbies)
**Lesson:** Lesson 8 — Hobby (2): Sports 运动 | Lesson 1 of 8
**Duration:** 50 minutes

## Learning Intention
We are learning to name sports in Chinese and choose the correct verb (打 / 踢 / no verb) for each one.

## Success Criteria
- I can say 运动, 跑步, 游泳, 网球, 打网球 in Chinese (拼音说出核心运动词汇)
- I can use 跟……一起 to say who I do something with (跟朋友一起打网球)
- I can pick the right verb pattern: 打 for racket sports, no verb for 跑步/游泳

## Materials Needed
- Projector (vocab slides: 汉字 + pinyin + English)
- Mini whiteboards + markers
- Printed vocab-tracing worksheet (New Words Text 1)
- Bingo grids (pre-made, today's 7 words)

---

## Lesson Sequence

### Review (0–8 min)
**What:** Lesson 7 (Music) vocabulary and 一边……一边…… — 弹钢琴, 唱歌, 听音乐, 读书, 都, 正在
**How:** Flashcard rapid fire — teacher shows a Lesson 7 word on the projector, class chorally says pinyin + English. Then teacher says an English hobby, students write the Chinese on mini whiteboards and hold up.
**Connection:** "Last unit we talked about music hobbies with 一边……一边……. Today we start a new hobby topic — 运动 (sports) — and you'll meet a new way to say who you do something *with*."

### Learning Intention & Success Criteria (8–10 min)
Display on projector. Read the learning intention aloud together. For each success criterion, ask "hands up if you've heard this word before" — 网球 and 游泳 are common loanwords/cognates some students may already know.

### I Do (10–20 min)
#### New Vocabulary
| 汉字 | 拼音 | English |
|---|---|---|
| 运动 | yùndòng | sports; exercise |
| 跑步 | pǎobù | run; jog |
| 游泳 | yóuyǒng | swim |
| 一起 | yìqǐ | together |
| 跟……一起 | gēn…yìqǐ | together with |
| 网球 | wǎngqiú | tennis |
| 打网球 | dǎ wǎngqiú | play tennis |

Introduce each word: 汉字 → pinyin → English, class repeats twice (choral). Keep pinyin visible throughout. Point out 跑 (run) and 步 (step) combine literally as "run-step" = jog; 游 (swim) + 泳 (swim) double up for emphasis — Chinese often pairs near-synonym characters like this.

Teach the **verb rule** explicitly: 网球 needs 打 in front (打网球 — "hit tennis"), but 跑步 and 游泳 never take a verb in front — they're already complete action-words. Write two columns on the board: "打 + ball sport" vs "no verb — already a verb." Tell students more sports (and the 踢 category) are coming next lesson.

#### Key Sentence Pattern
> 我喜欢 + sport. / 我喜欢跟朋友一起 + sport.
> 我喜欢跑步。/ 我喜欢游泳。/ 我喜欢跟朋友一起打网球。

Model 3 examples, thinking aloud: "To say 'I like swimming with my friend', I don't need 打 — 我喜欢跟朋友一起游泳。" Insert a silly one: 我喜欢跟我的猫一起打网球！(pause for reactions — cats don't play tennis).

#### CFU Checkpoints
- "What's the verb for 网球? Thumbs up if you remember it's 打."
- "How do you say 'swim' in Chinese? Write it on your whiteboard."
- "Error check: 我打跑步。What's wrong?" (check: 跑步 needs no verb — should be 我跑步)

### Flexible Practice (20–43 min)

#### Activity 1: Vocab Tracing & Matching Worksheet — We Do (8 min)
Students trace each of the 7 new words three times on the printed worksheet, then complete a matching column (汉字 ↔ pinyin ↔ English) for all 7.
- **Scaffold:** Pinyin printed above each character on the tracing lines; matching column includes a partially-filled example.
- **Extension:** After matching, students cover the English column and self-quiz by writing meanings from memory.
- **OTR:** Every student writes every word — 100% response rate by design.

#### Activity 2: Verb-Rule Sorting on Whiteboards — We Do → You Do (8 min)
Teacher calls out a sport (跑步 / 游泳 / 网球). Students write either "打 + word" or "no verb" on their whiteboard and hold up on the count of three.
- **Scaffold:** Two-column reference chart stays projected (打 column / no-verb column).
- **Extension:** Once confident, students must also write the full sentence with 我喜欢, not just the verb choice.
- **OTR:** Whole-class simultaneous whiteboard hold-up — teacher scans for the most common error and re-teaches on the spot.

#### Activity 3: Sentence-Building — You Do (7 min)
Using the frame 我喜欢____。/ 我喜欢跟朋友一起____。, students write 3 original sentences combining today's vocabulary, swapping in at least one 跟……一起 sentence.
- **Scaffold:** Sentence frame printed with a word bank (跑步, 游泳, 打网球) alongside.
- **Extension:** Students add a second clause using 一边……一边…… from last unit (我喜欢一边跑步一边听音乐 — even if not literally sensible, it's valid practice).
- **OTR:** Cold call 4–5 students to read one sentence aloud before moving to the game.

#### Game: Bingo (5 min)
Students fill a 3×3 grid with today's 7 words (their choice of which 7 squares — some repeat). Teacher calls out English meanings or pinyin one at a time; students cross off the matching 汉字. First to a full line calls 宾狗 (Bingo).
- **Setup needed:** Blank grids (photocopied), markers/pens.

### Plenary (43–50 min)
- **Revisit Success Criteria:** Student volunteer reads each criterion aloud; class responds with the target word/sentence.
- **Self-assessment:** Thumbs up / sideways / down against each of the 3 criteria.
- **Exit ticket:** "Write one sentence saying you like a sport with a friend, using 跟……一起."
- **Preview:** "Next lesson we're learning 8 more sports — basketball, football, skiing, and more — and practising 跟……一起 properly."
