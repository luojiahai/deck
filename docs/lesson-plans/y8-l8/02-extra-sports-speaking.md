# Lesson 8.2: More Sports + 跟(和)……一起

**Year Level:** Year 8
**Unit:** Unit 3 — 爱好 (Hobbies)
**Lesson:** Lesson 8 — Hobby (2): Sports 运动 | Lesson 2 of 8
**Duration:** 50 minutes

## Learning Intention
We are learning to name more sports in Chinese and say who we do them with using 跟(和)……一起.

## Success Criteria
- I can name at least 8 sports in Chinese (排球, 篮球, 乒乓球, 羽毛球, 高尔夫球, 足球, 滑冰, 滑雪)
- I can use 跟(和)……一起 to make a full sentence about doing a sport with someone
- I can explain why 足球 uses 踢 and 滑冰/滑雪 use no verb at all

## Materials Needed
- Projector (8 sport picture-word slides)
- Mini whiteboards + markers
- Sport flashcards (one set per pair, for chain drill and Slap the Board)

---

## Lesson Sequence

### Review (0–8 min)
**What:** Lesson 1 vocabulary and the 打/no-verb rule — 跑步, 游泳, 网球, 打网球, 跟……一起
**How:** Pair dialogue — students recreate the 我喜欢跟朋友一起打网球 pattern from memory with a partner, then swap roles and vary the sport.
**Connection:** "Yesterday you learned 3 sports and one verb rule. Today you're getting 8 more sports — and a second verb, 踢, for football."

### Learning Intention & Success Criteria (8–10 min)
Display on projector. Ask: "Which of these 8 sports do you think you already know the English cognate for?" (乒乓球 = ping pong, 高尔夫球 = golf) to build confidence before teaching.

### I Do (10–20 min)
#### New Vocabulary
| 汉字 | 拼音 | English |
|---|---|---|
| 打排球 | dǎ páiqiú | play volleyball |
| 打篮球 | dǎ lánqiú | play basketball |
| 打乒乓球 | dǎ pīngpāngqiú | play table tennis |
| 打羽毛球 | dǎ yǔmáoqiú | play badminton |
| 打高尔夫球 | dǎ gāo'ěrfūqiú | play golf |
| 踢足球 | tī zúqiú | play football / soccer |
| 滑冰 | huábīng | ice skate |
| 滑雪 | huáxuě | ski |

Show each word, model pronunciation twice, class repeats twice (choral). Point out 球 (ball) appears in almost every one — "ball" is the shared radical clue across this whole vocabulary set.

Complete the verb rule chart from Lesson 1 by adding two more columns: 踢 (足球 only — a kicking sport) and no-verb (滑冰, 滑雪 — like 跑步 and 游泳, these are already complete verbs). Three categories total now: 打 / 踢 / no verb.

#### Key Sentence Pattern
> 我喜欢跟(和)朋友一起 + sport.
> 我喜欢跟朋友一起打篮球。/ 我哥哥喜欢和同学一起踢足球。/ 我们喜欢跟家人一起滑雪。

Model 3 examples, each with a different subject (我 / 我哥哥 / 我们) to show the pattern doesn't change. Silly example: 我喜欢跟我的狗一起打乒乓球！(pause for reactions).

#### CFU Checkpoints
- "Quick translate: 'play basketball'. Write it." (check: 打篮球)
- "Error correction: 我打足球。What's wrong?" (check: should be 踢足球, not 打足球)
- "Thumbs up if 滑雪 needs a verb in front of it." (check: no thumbs — it needs none)

### Flexible Practice (20–43 min)

#### Activity 1: Choral + TPR Picture Drill — We Do (7 min)
Teacher projects each of the 8 sport pictures in random order; class chorally calls out the Chinese word, then mimes the action (swing a racket, kick, skate).
- **Scaffold:** Pinyin stays visible under each picture for the first pass.
- **Extension:** On the second pass, hide pinyin — students rely on the picture and character only.
- **OTR:** Whole-class choral response + mime on every picture.

#### Activity 2: Pair Grammar Drill — 跟(和)……一起 (Act. 2) — We Do → You Do (9 min)
In pairs, students complete 4 sentence starters using 跟(和)……一起 (e.g. 我喜欢____一起打篮球 → 我喜欢跟妹妹一起打篮球), then write 4 fully original sentences of their own using different sports and different people (朋友/哥哥/同学/家人).
- **Scaffold:** Word bank of people (朋友, 哥哥, 姐姐, 同学, 家人) and today's 8 sports projected as reference.
- **Extension:** Students add a reason or frequency word from memory (经常, 每个周末) if they recall it from context — flagged as "bonus, not required yet."
- **OTR:** Turn and talk — each partner reads one sentence aloud to the other before sharing with the class.

#### Activity 3: Chain Drill — Naming 10 Sports (Act. 6) — You Do (7 min)
Students stand in a circle or row. First student says one sport in Chinese; next student repeats it and adds a new one; chain continues until someone can't recall the sequence or a new sport, aiming for 10 total sports (today's 8 + 网球 + 打网球's counterpart from Lesson 1).
- **Scaffold:** Sport flashcards laid out on the table as a visual prompt for anyone who blanks.
- **Extension:** Advanced students must also add 跟朋友一起 before each sport, not just the bare word.
- **OTR:** Every student in the chain must produce at least one sport aloud.

#### Game: Slap the Board (5 min)
Two students at the board (or a table with cards spread out) — teacher calls a sport in Chinese, both slap the matching card/word. Winner stays for next round.
- **Setup needed:** Sport cards printed and spread on a table, or written on the whiteboard in a grid.

### Plenary (43–50 min)
- **Revisit Success Criteria:** Choral read from the board; class calls out one example sport per criterion.
- **Self-assessment:** Show of hands — 👍/😐/👎 against each criterion.
- **Exit ticket:** "Write one sentence: who do you play a sport with? Use 跟(和)……一起."
- **Preview:** "Next lesson we'll read Gao Li's family sports story and practise the tricky ou/uo sounds."
