## Lesson 4: Radicals + Pinyin Listening

**Option: A — Scaffolded step-by-step**

### Learning Intention
We are learning the 6 new radicals for Lesson 12 and practising the pinyin finals ang/eng/ing/ong.

### Success Criteria
- I can name the 6 radicals and give an example character for each (说出6个部首和例字)
- I can identify the meaning clue a radical gives (说出部首的意思)
- I can listen and write pinyin with the correct final and tone (听写拼音)

### Materials Needed
- Projector
- Mini whiteboards
- Radical flashcards, printed
- Radical puzzle cards (Ex12, p.94), printed
- Pinyin listening list (CD T56, or teacher-read if audio unavailable)

---

### Lesson Sequence

#### Review (0–8 min)

**What:** Lesson 3's 上/下 phrase pairs.

**How:** Slap the Board — two students at the board with 8 phrase cards spread out (上课/下课/上学/放学/上班/下班/上车/下车); teacher says the English meaning, students race to slap the correct phrase.

**Connection:** "You've got the phrase pairs down. Today we go under the hood — the radicals inside these Lesson 12 characters — and train your ear for new pinyin sounds."

#### Learning Intention & Success Criteria (8–10 min)
Project, choral read, keep visible.

#### I Do (10–20 min)

##### Radicals (p.89, Exercise 4)
| 部首 | Meaning | Example character |
|---|---|---|
| 文 | writing | 数 (shù, number/count) |
| 欠 | owe | 欢 (huān, happy) |
| 士 | scholar | 喜 (xǐ, joy) |
| 门 | door | 问 (wèn, ask) |
| 竹 | bamboo | 等 (děng, wait) |
| 钅 | metal | 铁 (tiě, iron — recall 地铁 from Lesson 2!) |

**Scaffolded, one radical at a time:** for each radical — show it alone → show it highlighted inside the example character → say what image/meaning it evokes → students copy-write the radical 3 times on whiteboards → move to the next. Do not introduce all 6 at once. Demonstrate stroke order on the projector for the two trickier ones (竹, 钅) before students copy.

##### CFU Checkpoints (embedded per radical)
- After 门: "What does 门 mean? Thumbs up if you know." (door)
- After 钅: "Which Lesson 12 word do you know with 钅 in it?" (地铁)
- Quick check: "Write the radical for 'bamboo'." (竹) — on whiteboards.

##### Pinyin Exercise 5 (p.89, CD T56) — finals ang/eng/ing/ong
Model 2 example words first (teacher says, writes the pinyin with the correct tone mark), thinking aloud about the final sound.

#### Flexible Practice (20–43 min)

##### Activity 1: Guided Radical Copying + Matching — We Do (8 min)
Students receive a worksheet with the 6 radicals and 6 example characters scrambled. With teacher guidance, they draw a line matching radical to character, then copy each radical 3× in the correct stroke order.

- **Scaffold:** Radical meanings printed alongside for reference throughout.
- **Extension:** Find one more character already known from earlier units that contains one of these 6 radicals, and write it down.
- **OTR:** Mini whiteboards — hold up matched pairs each round.

##### Activity 2: Pinyin Listening Dictation — You Do (8 min)
Teacher reads 6–8 words containing ang/eng/ing/ong finals (Ex5, CD T56 or teacher-read); students write the pinyin with tone marks on whiteboards, hold up after each.

- **Scaffold:** A bank of the possible finals (ang, eng, ing, ong) displayed on the projector as reference.
- **Extension:** Also write the tone number (1st/2nd/3rd/4th) for each word.
- **OTR:** Whiteboards every round.

##### Game: Radical Puzzle Relay (Ex12, p.94) (5 min)
Relay race: radical cards and incomplete-character cards spread at the front; students run up one at a time to match a radical card to the correct incomplete character, forming a complete character. Team with the most correct matches wins.

- **Setup needed:** Printed radical cards + incomplete character cards.

#### Plenary (43–50 min)
- **Revisit Success Criteria:** Choral read; 👍/😐/👎.
- **Self-assessment:** On whiteboards: which radical do you still confuse with another?
- **Exit ticket:** "Write the radical in 铁 and say what it means." (钅, metal)
- **Preview:** "Next lesson: we put time AND transport together in full sentences — like 'At 7:30, dad drives to work.'"
